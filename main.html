Background

nature’s inexhaustible sources of energy—sun, wind, and tide...”​


In the recent years there has been an increase concern for the utilization of sustainable energy resources and the same has been introduced in the school syllabus.
I always thought whether tidal energy could be harnessed in India and whether the favorable conditions for harnessing such energy resource is available. Last year during my visit to Puri,
I noticed huge waves on the sea shore and the idea struck my mind that if the energy of these waves could be harnessed then a large amount of electricity could be generated which would reduce our dependence on coal and petroleum products.​

This includes about 7,000 MW in the Gulf of Cambay in Gujarat, 1,200 MW in the Gulf of Kutch and 800 MW in the Gangetic delta in the Sunder bans region of West Bengal and geographically I noticed that Puri lies near the Utkal Plains, coastal plains in eastern Odisha state, and the plains are bounded by the Lower Ganges (Ganga) Plain to the north, the Bay of Bengal on the east, the Tamil Nadu Plains to the south, and the Eastern Ghats to the west.​

Technical Report



Concept and Theory


Description of concepts, theories and/or approach involved in the proposed solution:​
Concept:​
Wave caught through multiple segment propeller to rotate the alternator for generation of electricity.​
Theory: ​
Energy is the quantitative property that must be transferred to an object in order to perform work. According to the principal of conservation of energy; Energy can neither be create nor destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to other. In this case the mechanical energy of the sea waves is converted into electrical energy through an alternator.



Technical aspect of the proposed solution​


The technical aspects of my plant are:​
a) Surveying of the sea coasts for suitable strength and height of the tides and depth of the coast for setting up plant.​
b) Conversion of the frequency and voltage of generated electricity through transformer and frequency converter to match with the required grid voltage and frequency.​
c) After mechanical load test amount of electricity generation can be decided.​
d) If the geographical conditions permit multiple power stations can be setup one after the other to get the maximum electricity.​



FLOW CHART


Description of the flow of operations demonstrating key features and functionality


a) Wave height will be considered for designing the system and accordingly the system’s height shall be designed and it would be based on the various wave heights as per survey report. Wave period shall be 24x7x365 days or you can say throughout the year all time.​
b) The fixed structure mentioned in the schematic is a space frame that supports the shaft at many places. At the end of every propeller shaft there is a shaft support with roller bearing. Span of the support may be in between 10 to 20 meter which implies that the support distance depends on the length of the propeller or propeller shaft length. (Refer FIG-4).
c)Structure slope is adjustable based on requirement. It can vary in between 5 degree to 30 degree approx. and is needed to be finalized in such a way that the slope propeller can get maximum thrust. The shore side of the structure end will be hinged type and the sea side will be adjustable slope fixing support.​
d) Design of such space frame to withstand wave forces will be challenging and has been taken into account in my design.​
e) All turbine wheels shall rotate at same speed because all turbine shafts are mechanically coupled with each other as well as with generator gear box.​


Performance estimate of the solution


a) A mechanical load test has to be performed instead of placing alternator along with gear box and as per mechanical load test report; we need to decide alternator size along with MW rating. Weight and speed of the mechanical load is the key factor for further decision making and to reduce the weight of the assembly, Aluminum component to be used where ever possible for example shaft, propeller etc.​
I) Approximate weight of space frame per segment = 4 tones​
II) Approximate weight of propeller and shaft=0.4 tones​
III) Approximate weight of alternator with all components (like gear box) =3 tones​

b) Turbine wheel shall face two side waves. One is front wave and another is return water wave. Front wave height is very high compare to return wave. So, radius of the turbine wheel shall be from shaft to return wave height.​
I) The height/radius of the turbine wheel=1 to 2.5 meter​
II) The length of the shaft= 5-10 meter​

Result


Pros and Cons of your solution:​

Pros:​

1. Extremely low cost of electricity per unit which is beneficial for the consumer as the below graph depicts the average electricity cost per kilowatt hour. It is clearly evident that this amount is continuously increasing and was calculated as Rs. 6.09 per kilowatt hour in the fiscal year 2019. This amount can be reduced by harnessing the seamless tidal energy .​​

2. Negligible pollution and eco-friendly.​
3. Electricity can be made available 24X7 unlike solar energy which can be used only during the daytime.​
4. It will act as a source of endless power as tides depend on the gravitational attraction of the sun and moon, which is not going to end anytime soon.​
5. It has low construction cost and is only one-time investment.​

Cons:​

1. Power may vary from high to low due to variation in the intensity of tides.​
2. Synchronizing speed of the alternator cannot be kept constant due to variation in tides.​
3. AC power cannot be directly generated due to variation in synchronizing speed.​
4. Not resistant to natural calamities like Cyclone and Typhoon.​
